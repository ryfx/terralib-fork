Building Normal Probability Graphics

The normal probability graphic is another type of graphic created by TerraView. This graphic is useful to verify the validity of the assumption that a sample comes from a normal distribution. Use the popup menu from SetoresCensitarios theme and select Graphic Parameters to open the graphic definition interface. Choose Graphic Type as Normal Probability and in Attribute(X) select the number of males between zero and 5 years of age (H_0_5). In Objects field, keep All option to use all data in the graphic.

The normal probability graphic, where one coordinate (x) is presented in arithmetic scale and the other (y) is presented in cumulative percentage, is a way to visualize if the values are consistent with a normal distribution. The graphic presents the sample data in blue line and the line related to normal distribution in red. In such way, the closer the blue line is to the red straight line, the higher is the confidence on the normality assumption for sample data:



Observe that the blue line representing the data is very close to the red line representing a normal distribution and that they are almost coincident. Therefore, the normality assumption for the number of males over 45 years of age in each BH census tract is highly significant.

To proceed to next chapters and obtain identical results, apply the following changes:

1. In popup menu of SetoresCensitarios theme select Visual > Default..., choose Style as Transparent and click OK;

2. In popup menu of SetoresCensitarios theme select Remove Colors > Pointed Objects.

Thematic maps

Thematic maps are used to color maps based on important attributes and define categories for objects in accordance to colors. For instance, homicides may be categorized by sex or age of the victim, municipalities may be categorized by population, number of kidnappings, or kidnappings rate.

In TerraView, the drawing elements associated with objects attributes displayed on the map (different colors, pie charts) are called Theme Legend and are represented in the Views tree under the related theme.
